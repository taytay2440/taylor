A Guide to Cheap Eats in NYC

For those visiting New York City on a budget, planning where to eat can be a daunting task. With high prices at every turn, here are the top 15 places to eat around New York City all for under $15.

1. Mamoun's Falafel - Mamoun's Falafel is one of the oldest falafel restaurants in NYC, having been open since 1971. Today it is still family owned and operated and also offers some food. Mamoun’s offers sandwiches, plates, and kebobs, all with meat or vegetarian. The prices range between $3.75 for a vegetarian sandwich, to $12 for a meat plate.

Must try: Shawarma meat plate $12

30 St. Marks Place, New York, NY, 10003

2. Joe’s Pizza - Pizza and New York City go hand in hand. While there is basically a pizza place on every block, Joe’s Pizza stands out from the rest. Though the outside may seem no different from the other pizza shops, the lines and walls of celebrity photos should give you a clue that this place is special. Slices range between $2.75-$3.50, while whole pies run $20-$24.

Must try: A slice of white pizza - $3.00

7 Carmine St., New York, NY 10014

3. Shake Shack - Shake Shack is one those places where you just can’t go wrong. This NYC staple offers burgers, hot dogs, milkshakes, and some of the best frozen custards around. Burgers here run between $4.55 and $9, while all other menu items are below $10. An added bonus is that they also offer treats for dogs.

Must try: Frisky Dog $3.50 (hot dog topped with spicy tomato-simmered onions and kraut.)

Madison Square Park, Madison Ave & E.23rd St., New York, NY 10010

4. Vanessa’s Dumpling House - For those moments when you’re down to your last few dollars, Vanessa’s is the perfect choice. One of the best deals on the menu is four dumplings for just $1.50. In NYC this is one of the lowest prices around. You can also pick up a 50-piece frozen bag for just $12. Besides dumplings, Vanessa’s also offers noodle dishes, soups, and sesame pancakes. All for under $8.00.

Must try: 8 shrimp dumplings $5.00

118A Eldridge St., New York, NY 10002

5. Num Pang Sandwich Shop - This cambodian style sandwich shop doesn’t just offer a wide variety of sandwiches. On their menu they also have soups, salads, rice bowls and noodle bowls. The entire menu is also under $13. Their sandwich selection has everything from peppercorn catfish and grilled steak, to roasted cauliflower and barbecue brisket.

Must try: Coconut tiger shrimp sandwich $9.25

140 E 41st St., New York, NY 10017

6. Totto Ramen - While this place may be tiny on the outside, their portions are anything but small. One of the best parts of Totto Ramen is that you can customize your own bowl with a number of different toppings. They also offer speciality ramnes that vary by location. If you decide to stick to the menu, all the ramen is $12 and under, and appetizers range between $3-$7.

Must try: Spicy Paitan - $12 (Chicken Paitan topped with house made spicy sesame oil, scallions, bean sprouts, and nori.)

366 W. 52nd St., New York, NY 10019

7. Murray’s Bagels - Whether you’re just visiting New York, or a born and raised New Yorker, everyone needs a good New York City bagel from time to time. Murray’s is the go-to bagel spot. Here you’ll find quite the variety of bagels, cream cheese, deli meats, and sandwiches. You can also stop in for breakfast to grab one of the many bagel-based options. Bagels start at $1.25, and go to $11.50 for sandwiches.

Must try: The Red Hook Brooklyn sandwich - $9.75 (smoked turkey or homemade roast beef, Swiss cheese, roasted pepper)

500 6th Ave., New York, NY 10011

8. Baohaus - Bao is a steamed Chinese bread roll with a meat or veggie filling. At Baohaus, Evan and Eddie Huang takes baos to the next level. You’ll find options with fried chicken, pork belly, and fried tofu, all ranging between $3.55-$7.50. Also on the menu are rice bowls ($10-12) and unique fries including taro and sweet bao ($3.55).

Must try: Chairman Bao® $4.05 (Braised all natural Berkshire pork belly served with Haus Relish, crushed peanuts, Taiwanese red sugar, and cilantro)

238 East 14th St., New York, NY 10003

9. Crif Dogs - One of the late night spots on the list, Crif Dogs is open until 2 am. The hot dogs here are made with naturally smoked beef and pork, and all range between $5 and $6. Here you can get classic chili cheese dogs and corn dogs, or try some of their wilder options like the chiwawa, a bacon wrapped hot dog with avocado and sour cream.

Must try: The Jon-Jon Deragon $6.00 (a hot dog with cream cheese, scallions, and everything bagel seeds.)

113 St. Marks Place, New York, NY 10009

10. Bobwhite Lunch and Supper Counter - Southern fried chicken is the main event at Bobwhite Lunch and Supper Counter. This East Village luncheonette is full of cheap options for lunch and dinner. One of the specialities is the $11.25 “Chicken Little”, which includes three pieces of fried chicken, a buttermilk biscuit, and a side of your choice.

Must try: Banana pudding $6.00

94 Avenue C, New York, NY 10009

11. Teremok - Teremok offers classic Russian dishes with a modern twist. The restaurant specializes in bliny, thin Russian pancakes stuffed with sweet or savory fillings. Some savory options include ham and cheese, chicken and apple cream, and lox and sour cream. On the sweet side they offer dark chocolate, dulce de leche, and maple syrup. In addition to the bliny, they also serve traditional Russian soups, salads and breakfast. The price is also hard to beat, with their entire menu under $10!

Must try: Dulce de leche bliny with apples $4.95

358 7th Ave., New York, NY 10001

12. Sons of Thunder - This casual eatery aims to bring West Coast inspired food to New York. Their eclectic menu brings together poke bowls, hot dogs, cheese fries, and handspun milk shakes. The prices here range from $11.50 for a poke bowl, and under $6 for a hotdog. An added bonus is that Sons of Thunder also serves a number of vegetarian options.

Must try: Tako Octopus poke $11.50

204 E 38th St., New York, NY 10016

13. Chirping Chicken - Chirping Chicken is another great chain with locations throughout New York. This place specializes in anything chicken. From full or half chickens, to chicken sandwiches, wings and salads, they have it all. If you aren’t a huge fan of chicken, they also offer burgers, gyros, and ribs. One of the best deals is the half chicken meal which also includes a salad, pita bread and your choice of side. This rings in at $13.75

Must try: Arroz con pollo $10

1560 2nd Ave., New York, NY 10028

14. Halal Guys - Over the past few years the Halal Guys have become an international restaurant chain. With locations across the United States, South Korea and the Philippines, it’s easy to forget that the Halal Guys were once just a simple food cart. While a lot has changed since then, the low prices have remained the same. Here you can get meat platters for $6.99, and sandwiches for $5.49. This is one of those places that will leave you stuffed. An essential element to any Halal Guys order is their white sauce. While the actual recipe remains shrouded in mystery, the flavor is unbeatable.

Must try: Beef gyro platter $6.99

W 53rd Street & 7th Ave.,New York, NY 10019

15. Xi'an Famous Foods - This New York chain of Northern Chinese food is hard to beat. This family owned business aims to introduce the United States to their regional cuisine. With soups and noodle dishes all under $12, and burgers for under $6, Xi’an Famous Food is a must try. Besides the cheap prices, the place offers a great selection of Chinese dishes with an unbeatable flavor.

Must try: Spicy cumin lamb burger - $5.69

328 E 78th St., New York, NY 10075
